Wednesday, December 31, 2003 -
 
Happy New Year!

Drop Trio would like to take this moment and say THANK YOU to all of our friends and supporters! We wouldn't be anywhere without you. In the past year, we've played over 80 shows, recorded and released a full length studio album, won awards from the Houston Press, and traveled all over Texas (and beyond) spreading the message of instrumental funk jazz.

The coming year holds plenty of surprises, celebrations, and most importantly, chances to play music for you all. We've got tons of new material in the wings, and we expect to start work on our next record sometime this spring. We're also expecting a new member of the family - Nino's daughter is due in May, so expect to hear plenty more about that (as well as shows with guest bassists, including Marc Reczek, Thomas Helton, Guy Schwartz, Adrian Zavala, and more ...)

Happy new year, we'll see you soon!

Drop Trio tunes are now available through a new online record label called Magnatune. Their slogan is "We are not evil", and they mean it:
We call it "try before you buy." It's the shareware model applied to music. Listen to hundreds of MP3'd albums from our artists. Or try our genre-based radio stations.

If you like what you hear, buy our music online for as little as $5 an album or license our music for commercial use. Artists get a full 50% of the purchase price. And unlike most record labels, our artists keep the rights to their music.

Founded by musicians, for musicians. No major label connections. We are not evil.
You can listen to our tracks from Big Dipper and buy the tracks digitally online. Also check out some of the "Customers who bought ..." picks on our page - I'm listening to some now, and they're awesome!

Monday, December 15, 2003 -
 
Generosity.

There's lots of that in the air this weekend in Houston. Thanks to all who took part. The Alejandro Escovedo benefit was a big success - no final numbers yet, but I know there were at least 180 people at the door, plus a few large sum donations. And we flooded the House Rules station with votes - lots of people told me they were voting. We'll find out if Cindy won next Friday night.

Till then, please accept our thanks and the thanks of our friends, for helping out in a big way this weekend.

Wednesday, December 03, 2003 -
 
Some friends of ours are doing a show at Helios (411 Westheimer @ Taft) tonight. Check it out:
Secret Beat Society presents the NEW BEATNIK REVUE - Poetry and hip-hop deep fried and spiced with the flavor of the south. Hosted by Lyric Osiris with DJ MR.E.

This week the Secret Beat Society will feature a perfomance by The Last Soul Descendents (acid jazz, hip-hop, dub, drum-n-bass). Starts at 11pm. $3 cover.
Drop Trio has yet to do a show at Helios (not sure why!) but it's a great venue for checking out Houston's best new jazz, groove, hip hop, etc. There is always great stuff going on there, and tonight is no exception. The Last Soul Descendents have some of the most creative and deep downtempo / dub beats, and the most beautiful singing. You can always catch Darren & Leah, the core of the group; and if you're lucky, you can also hear Dionne Banks and Diva Karina.

The Secret Beat Society is fantastic - an interdisciplinary neo-beat collective, part poetry and part music, with poets and musicians spanning a spectrum of styles - classical to slam - hip-hop to jazz. Conspirators include Mike McGuire, Kool B, Lyric, and Ragah El ... definitely some of the coolest stuff going on in Houston these days, so go check it out!
