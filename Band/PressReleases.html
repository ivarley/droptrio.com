Drop Trio Brings Spaceship Jazz to SXSW

SXSW Showcase: Elephant Room, Thursday 3/16 @ 1am

Feb 24, 2006

A statistical breakdown of the 1300+ bands playing this year's South By Southwest Music festival would show that compared to Indie Rock (51.6%), Singer / Songwriters (9.8%), and Alt-Country (7.7%), the genre Spaceship Jazz is drastically under-represented - coming in at less than 0.1%.

Doing their part to right this imbalance is Texas' Drop Trio. Formed in 2002, the instrumental band has played relentlessly across the southern United States, inspiring legions of fans with their improvised prog-rock jazz. A bevy of media awards (such as Houston Press' "Best Jazz" and "Album Of The Year") put the trio on local radar, and frequent out-of-state trips promise to broaden their reach nationally.

Describing their music as "Herbie Hancock meets E.T.", the trio welds together influences as diverse as Steve Reich and P-Funk. "George Clinton spaceships? You bet," said bassist Patrick Flanagan. "But too, it's Brian Eno spaceships, ELP spaceships, and Sun Ra spaceships."

Drop Trio has released three albums, the latest a live recording from Houston's prestigious jazz venue, Cezanne. Each release has seen Drop Trio evolve, and their latest voyages put them squarely in the domain of spaceships - heavy doses of analog synthesizers and vintage organs, mixed with funk jazz beats and a careening, mixed-meter, composition-heavy approach.

"Basically, we're thinking about the future," said keyboardist Ian Varley. "We're into the future, and finding our way to it from the past."

"I guess it's basically just the present we're not into," added drummer Nuje Blattel. "Present = boring. Next question."

The band will be headlining at the venerable Austin jazz club, The Elephant Room (315 Congress), on Thursday, March 16th, at 1am. For more details, visit http://www.sxsw.com
